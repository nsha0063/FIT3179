World Population Statistics

Population Density Map

Population Density is a measurement of the population per unit of land area, in this case, the number of people per square km for each country.

Australia's Population Density

From the map, Australia is seen to only have a population density of 3 ppl/sq km, despite having a population of around 25 million people, due to the large land area of around 8 million sq km.

Countries with the highest Population Density

The countries with the highest population densities include Bangladesh, South Korea, Netherlands and India whilst the countries with the lowest population densities include Australia, Iceland, Mongolia and Canada.

Freedom Ratings Chart

Freedom ratings track the quality of political rights and civil liberties for each country from the past decade. They are composed of two main rankings, Civil Liberties and Political Rights. The Civil Liberties (CL) rating is composed of 15 civil liberties indicators and the Political Rights (PR) rating is composed of 10 political rights indicators, with the lower rankings indicating a better overall freedom ranking. In the chart, the average CL and PR ratings for each continent are shown for comparison between different continents.

Oceania and Europe

Both the Oceania and Europe continents have had relatively high rankings in freedom ratings that remained consistent over the years 2013 till 2022. For Oceania during the year 2013, PR Ranking was lower than Europe, however, over the following 2-4 years, the PR Rating improved to overtake Europe's ranking.

Africa and Asia

Africa and Asia both have had relatively low rankings when it comes to CL/PR ratings and have consistently remained low during the past decade. This could be due to the combination of corruption and poor governance within Africa and many civil society organisations being forced to disband or relocate throughout Asia over the years.

North America and South America

Both North America and South America have remained consistently high in PR/CL rankings during the past decade with the average rankings of around 2-3 in both ratings.

Life Expectancy and GDP per Capita

Life Expectancy

Life Expectancy is used as a measurement of the health of a country and estimates the number of years a newborn baby is expected to live. It takes into account current death rates and age as well as other factors and probabilities to determine the average age of a person when they die.

GDP per Capita

GDP per capita is calculated by dividing the total GDP of a country by its total population to give a measurement of a country's economic production per person. It is seen as a global measure for the wealth of a nation's people and provides an insight into the average living standards for each person in a country.

Life Expectancy and GDP per Capita

By examining the Life Expectancy and GDP per Capita simultaneously, the overall economic and health of people can be examined along with the quality of life, whilst also considering the average income per person. Looking at these simultaneously gives an insight into the economic prosperity and general health of each country.
